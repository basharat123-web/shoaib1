Patient Experience Research for a Healthcare Network

How NRG helped a major healthcare provider identify key drivers of patient satisfaction and implement targeted service improvements.

Healthcare
Online Survey + Exit Interviews
2,400 patients
6 weeks

Client Context

A regional healthcare network with 12 facilities across three states was experiencing declining patient satisfaction scores and increasing patient complaints. Leadership needed to understand the specific drivers of dissatisfaction and prioritise service improvement investments.

The Challenge

Healthcare satisfaction research presents unique challenges: patients are often emotionally vulnerable, survey fatigue is high, and the factors that drive satisfaction are complex — spanning clinical quality, wait times, staff interaction, facilities, and communication. The client needed a study that could identify which specific factors, across which facility types, were most strongly correlated with overall satisfaction and likelihood to recommend.

Research Approach

NRG designed a mixed-method study combining:

  • A structured online survey of 2,400 recent patients (200 per facility) measuring 35 satisfaction dimensions
  • Driver analysis identifying the 8 most important predictors of overall satisfaction
  • Cross-facility benchmarking identifying best-in-class performers
  • Verbatim coding of 6,000 open-ended comments identifying recurring themes

Key Findings

The research identified that staff communication quality (clarity, empathy, and proactive information-sharing) accounted for 42% of the variance in overall satisfaction — far more than wait times, which had been the organisation's primary focus. Facilities with above-average communication scores outperformed peers on NPS by 31 points.

Business Impact

Based on NRG's findings, the client redesigned its staff communication training programme and deployed targeted interventions at the five lowest-scoring facilities. Twelve months post-implementation, overall satisfaction scores improved by 18 points and patient complaints fell by 34%.
