B2B Decision-Maker Study for a Software Company

Understanding the buying process and decision criteria of IT managers at mid-market enterprises.

Technology (SaaS)
Online B2B Survey + In-depth Interviews
350 IT decision-makers
8 weeks

Client Context

A growing SaaS company with a strong product was struggling to convert mid-market enterprise prospects. Despite positive product reviews and competitive pricing, their pipeline was stalling at the evaluation stage. They needed to understand the B2B buying process from the buyer's perspective.

The Challenge

Reaching IT managers and directors at mid-market companies (100–2,000 employees) is challenging — they receive enormous volumes of vendor outreach and are protective of their time. ARG needed to access this audience and understand the nuances of their buying process, key objections, and competitive evaluation criteria.

Research Approach

ARG deployed its B2B research panel and specialist recruitment to access 350 qualified IT decision-makers. The study included a 20-minute online quantitative survey and 15 in-depth qualitative interviews exploring the complete buying journey.

Key Findings

The research revealed that 67% of IT managers began the evaluation process 6+ months before any vendor contact — during a self-directed information phase. Security compliance documentation and integration capability evidence were the two most-requested assets, yet both were absent or hard to find on the client's website.

Business Impact

The client restructured their website content and sales enablement materials based on ARG's findings. Within six months, trial-to-paid conversion rates increased by 28% and average sales cycle length decreased by 3 weeks.
