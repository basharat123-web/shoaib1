Cybersecurity User Risk Study for an Insurer

Quantifying user security behaviour and awareness to refine cyber insurance underwriting models.

Insurance & Cybersecurity
Online Survey + Behavioural Assessment
3,000 US internet users
10 weeks

Client Context

A major insurance company offering cyber insurance products needed to better understand the relationship between user security behaviours, awareness levels, and actual cyber risk. Existing actuarial models were based primarily on technical factors and lacked behavioural insight.

Research Approach

ARG designed a comprehensive behavioural assessment covering password hygiene, phishing susceptibility, software update practices, social engineering vulnerability, and security tool adoption. A stratified sample of 3,000 US internet users was surveyed across age, income, and employment categories.

Key Findings

The research identified four distinct user risk segments — from "Security Champions" (12%) to "High-Risk Inactives" (28%). Risk segment membership was more strongly predicted by security training history and awareness levels than by demographics — challenging the industry assumption that older users are always higher risk.

Business Impact

The client integrated ARG's behavioural risk typology into their underwriting model as supplementary questionnaire items. Initial validation showed the behavioural variables increased predictive accuracy for claims by 19% compared to the prior model.
